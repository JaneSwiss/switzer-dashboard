# target-audience.md — Switzertemplates

This file gives every agent a detailed picture of who the Switzertemplates
customer is - how she thinks, what she struggles with, what she wants,
and how to speak to her in a way that lands.

Read this before writing any content, email, caption, or pin copy.

---

## Who she is

Female small business owner - either service-based or product-based.
This includes coaches, consultants, therapists, wellness professionals,
beauty specialists, and online service providers, but also eCommerce
sellers, makers, and physical product businesses who need a professional
online store and brand presence.

She is typically in one of two stages:

**Stage 1 - just starting out or rebranding:**
She is setting up her business or realising her current brand doesn't
look the way she wants it to. She needs a complete foundation fast -
branding, website, social media presence - without spending a fortune
or figuring everything out herself.

**Stage 2 - established but stuck:**
She has been running her business for a while but her brand looks
inconsistent, her website is weak or non-existent, and she knows it's
holding her back. She is ready to invest in looking more professional
but doesn't want to start from scratch or hire an expensive agency.

---

## Demographics

- Female, typically 25-45 years old
- Runs or is building a service-based or product-based business
- Located in English-speaking markets (US, UK, Australia, Canada)
- Has a working knowledge of Canva and is comfortable using a computer
- Has a real, functioning business with existing revenue or clear
  momentum - not just an idea
- Values her time and is willing to invest money to save it
- May have a full-time job alongside her business, or has gone
  full-time into her own business

---

## The ideal customer - be specific when writing copy

Content and copy should attract the customer who:

- Already has a business up and running (or is launching imminently)
- Is comfortable using Canva and basic computer tools
- Understands that good branding and a professional website are
  investments, not expenses
- Has the budget to spend $15-$82 without it being a significant
  stretch
- Is looking for a quality shortcut - not the cheapest option available
- Appreciates premium design and can tell the difference between
  good and generic

When writing copy, speak to this person. She is not a beginner who
needs to be taught what Canva is. She is a business owner who has
been around long enough to know that her branding needs work.

---

## Who to avoid attracting (non-ideal customer)

Content and copy should not attract or encourage:

- People who are just "thinking about" starting a business with no
  real product or service yet
- People who have no familiarity with Canva or basic computer use -
  this will lead to support issues and poor reviews
- People who are struggling financially and are looking for the
  cheapest solution available - they will be disappointed by any
  minor issue and are unlikely to be satisfied customers
- People who expect a fully done-for-you service with no effort on
  their part - the products require the customer to customise and
  implement, which takes basic skills

Do not use language like "even if you have no experience", "perfect
for complete beginners", or "no skills needed at all." These phrases
attract the wrong audience. Instead use language that assumes
competence: "ready to customise", "easy to edit", "set up in days."

## Her business situation

- Selling services (coaching, consulting, therapy, beauty, wellness)
  or physical/digital products via eCommerce
- Active on Instagram, often Pinterest
- May or may not have a website - if she does, it often doesn't reflect
  her brand well
- Using Canva already for at least some content
- Probably on an email platform or thinking about starting one
- Revenue is there or growing but her brand doesn't reflect the level
  she is working at

---

## What she wants

- To look professional and credible online without spending months on it
- A cohesive brand that looks like it belongs to a real, established business
- More clients or sales - and she believes her current brand is holding
  her back
- A website that does the selling for her while she focuses on her work
- To stop wasting time on design and start showing up consistently
- To feel confident sharing her business online - not embarrassed by how
  it looks

---

## What she is afraid of

- Spending money on something that doesn't deliver the quality she imagines
- Looking like every other generic template business
- Not being able to customise it to fit her brand
- The tech being too complicated - especially Wix setup
- Making the wrong choice and wasting time

---

## The language she uses

When she talks about her problems, she says things like:

- "My brand just doesn't look professional enough"
- "I've been meaning to sort out my website for months"
- "I can never get my posts to look consistent"
- "I don't have time to design everything from scratch"
- "I want my business to look more legit"
- "I've been DIYing everything and it's starting to show"
- "I know I need a website but I don't know where to start"
- "I want to charge more but my brand doesn't match what I'm asking"

Use this language in copy wherever possible. When she reads the content
and thinks "that's exactly what I was thinking" - the copy is working.

---

## What makes her buy

She buys when she feels that:

1. The product looks exactly like what she needs (visual match)
2. It's easier and faster than she expected (ease angle)
3. Other people like her have already used it and been happy (social proof)
4. The price makes sense compared to the alternative (value framing)
5. She trusts the brand - it looks professional itself (brand credibility)

The 27,700+ sales and 2,800+ reviews are not just vanity numbers. For
this customer, they are the most important trust signal. Use them.

---

## What makes her hesitate

- "Will it look too generic?"
  - Address this by emphasising the premium, editorial design quality
    and the ability to customise colours, fonts, and photos
- "Is it too complicated to set up?"
  - Address this with the one-click Wix transfer angle and the fact
    that most customers are selling within a couple of days
- "Will it work for my niche?"
  - Address this by naming her niche specifically in copy where possible
    (coaches, therapists, wellness professionals, etc.)
- "What if I don't like it?"
  - Address this by directing her to the live preview before purchase

## Her relationship with design and tech

- Has working knowledge of Canva - she has used it before and is
  comfortable with the basics
- Comfortable using a computer and basic online tools
- Not a professional designer but has an eye for what looks good
- May be unfamiliar with Wix specifically but is not intimidated by
  technology in general
- Prefers templates that are ready to customise over starting from scratch
- Will update text, photos, and colours but does not want to rebuild layouts

**Important:** do not write copy that assumes zero tech knowledge.
She knows what Canva is. She has used it. Write to that level.

---

## Tone when speaking to her

- Speak to her like a knowledgeable peer, not a teacher or a salesperson
- Acknowledge her situation without condescending to her
- Be direct - she is busy and does not have time for long build-ups
- Make the benefit obvious immediately - she should know within the
  first two lines what this has to do with her
- Use "you" and "your" consistently - always centre her, not the product
- Empathy without being soft - she is a business owner, not a beginner
  who needs hand-holding
- Confidence in the product - the 27,700+ sales speak for themselves,
  write with that confidence behind every word

---

## What she responds to on Pinterest

- Clear, benefit-led titles that name what she gets or what problem is solved
- Visual examples of the actual templates (she wants to see before she clicks)
- Titles that speak to her specific situation: "for coaches", "for
  small business owners", "without a designer"
- Descriptions that make the purchase feel easy and low-risk

---

## What she responds to in email

- A subject line that sounds like it was written for her specifically
- An opening that acknowledges a real frustration she has had
- Practical content that gives her something useful before asking for anything
- A clear, specific CTA that tells her exactly what she is getting

---

## What she responds to on Instagram

- Before/after or transformation content
- Content that validates a frustration she has been silently carrying
- Educational content that makes her feel informed, not sold to
- Real, grounded copy - she is tired of overly polished brand speak
