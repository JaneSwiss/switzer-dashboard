# visual-style-guide.md — Switzertemplates

This file tells the Creative Designer agent exactly how all visual
content for Switzertemplates should look. Read this before generating
any pin, carousel, graphic, or mockup.

---

## Overall aesthetic

Minimal, editorial, feminine, and elevated.

The brand feels luxury-inspired without being flashy. Clean and polished,
with a soft neutral palette, warm earthy browns and beiges, and a strong
modern editorial / magazine-style feel.

Describe it as:
- Warm, neutral, and sophisticated
- Elegant and high-end
- Minimal with an editorial edge
- Soft luxury / quiet luxury
- Clean, refined, and feminine
- Modern business chic
- Timeless, polished, and premium
- Cohesive, muted, and aesthetic

What creates this look:
- Serif typography that feels stylish and editorial
- Neutral brown and beige tones that read as warm and premium
- Clean spacing with generous negative space - never cluttered
- Fashion-inspired imagery mixed with business content

## Typography

**Primary heading font:**
Noto Serif Display Light
- Used for main titles in lowercase
- Italic style can be applied to highlight key words within the title
- Can also be used in a combination of lowercase + all caps within
  the same title for stylistic contrast

**Secondary / supporting font:**
Montserrat Regular
- Used for secondary titles in all caps
- Clean, structured contrast to the serif heading

**Body text font:**
Montserrat Regular or DM Sans Regular
- Always lowercase for body text
- Used for descriptions, captions, and supporting copy on designs

**Typography rules:**
- Serif + sans combination gives the editorial feel - do not mix
  more than two fonts in one design
- Generous letter spacing on all-caps text
- Avoid bold weights unless specifically needed - the brand is light
  and refined, not heavy

## Pinterest pin design

### Background photo

Use business-related photography only:
- Female working on a laptop
- Office interiors
- Desktop flatlay
- Soft lifestyle / reading scenes
- Clean workspace or product imagery

**Photo treatment - apply all of the following:**
- Dark warm overlay to create a moody effect and make white text stand out
- Slightly darkened overall exposure
- Muted / reduced saturation
- Warm brown tint applied
- Soft contrast (not harsh)
- Subtle blur or softness in the background if needed

The overall result should feel warm, moody, and premium - not bright
or high-contrast.

### Composition

- The photo fills the full background of the pin
- The subject of the photo sits mostly in the upper or right side
- Text sits in the lower center-left area
- Generous negative space around all text elements
- Balance must feel clean and intentional - nothing crammed or busy
- Use a dark semi-transparent overlay layer over the photo before
  placing text, so white text is always readable

### Text on pins

- Main title: Noto Serif Display Light, white, lowercase (with selective
  italic for emphasis)
- Supporting text or keyword label: Montserrat Regular, all caps, white
  or soft cream
- Keep copy short - pins are not meant to carry long paragraphs
- One main message per pin

### Pin format

- Standard Pinterest pin ratio: 2:3 (1000 x 1500px recommended)
- Portrait orientation only

## Two pin design categories

**Category 1 - Educational content pins**
Reference style: https://www.pinterest.com/17thAve/wordpress-tips-tutorials/

These pins carry a tip, insight, or educational hook. The text is the
main focus. The photo provides mood and context. The design should feel
like something you would see in a premium online magazine.

**Category 2 - Product showcase pins**
Reference style: https://www.pinterest.com/switzertemplates/social-media-templates/

These pins show the actual product. Mockups, product previews, and
template screenshots are the hero of the design. The aesthetic should
match the product style - warm, clean, editorial.

## What to avoid

- Bright, saturated colours (red, pink, green, blue) unless explicitly
  requested
- Yellowish or greenish colour undertones anywhere in the design
- Cluttered layouts with too many elements
- Heavy or bold typography that feels harsh
- Generic stock photo aesthetics (overly posed, overly bright, plastic-looking)
- Drop shadows, heavy borders, or decorative frames that cheapen the look
- Mismatched fonts - stick to the defined typography system
- White backgrounds unless the product being showcased specifically
  calls for it
